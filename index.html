Professional Experience

I spent 4 and a half years working at Cerebralfix , a game studio doing work-for-hire before moving to the United Kingdom in June 2023. During my time at the studio, I worked mainly in the Interactive Experience space. At first, this was creating Web-based experiences using Phaser and Typescript, however, this quickly pivoted to Unreal Engine development of larger 3D experiences using C++.

My role at Cerebralfix expanded to being a Techincal Lead on various projects, this involved some of the following responsibilities:

  • Scoping incoming projects.
  • Tracking milestones and adjusting the roadmap as requirements changed.
  • Working with external developers and liaising with high-profile clients, such as Disney Parks.
  • Ensuring communication was high in the team and that everyone had clear goals and direction.

Unfortunately, large amounts of my work was under NDA so myself and Cerebralfix are unable to explicitly reference them. However, some of the projects I worked on that I can mention are …

Whale Watch Kaikōura Onboard Experience

This was my final project at CerebralFix and was another example of a successfully delivered project where I acted as Techincal Lead.

Some of the main technical challenges overcome during the development of this experience were:

  • Profiling and Optimising a third-party ocean plugin.
  • Investigating and Implementing Unreal Engine pixel streaming.
  • Creating a GeoTIFF parser plugin to turn the local terrain data into a Unreal Landscape mesh.
  • Creating a plugin to accept GPS data provided by a custom API, providing the ability to accurately place the vessel in the 3D level.
  • Custom scripting to enable deploying new builds and content versions remotely to the vessels.

See the Cerebralfix website for more detail

Adversity and Co (Virtual Production)

This was one of the major R&D efforts I was a part of at CerebralFix. When EPIC first released the Virtual Production tools under early access, it was decided that we would try and get a prototype working inside Unreal Engine. This was a real challenge as there was very little complete documentation and the functionality was not set up in Blueprints and templates the way it is today.

Nonetheless, we were able to get a working prototype in December 2021. Which was then further refined and used to create two short films, one of which won ‘Best VFX’ at the Poppy Jasper Film Festival in 2023.

See the Cerebralfix website for more detail

Rugby Runner

This was a very small 1-month project where I was a solo dev working with a part-time tester and artist. The scope of this game was a simple endless runner for a short marketing campaign. It followed OO design principle and had a basic CI pipeline for development. It was developed in Typescript using the HTML game engine Phaser . The reason it was developed in Phaser was because the game was predominantly a game for their website and Phaser works off a Canvas object which can be easily positioned on a web page.

We then used a tool called Cordova which hosted the web page and meant we could launch the game on the app stores.

Summer Internship Program

In 2022, I took on the responsibility to refresh and manage the internship program, giving a new set of students the opportunity to gain first-hand experience in the industry. This involved 4 developers and 1 artist working on a series of R&D-based projects in both Unreal Engine and Unity. These projects were run in the same way all projects at Cerebralfix were run giving the students the best insight into the industry as possible.
